What is PET/CT Scan?

Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and Computed Tomography (CT) scans are powerful imaging techniques individually. When combined in a PET/CT scan, they create a comprehensive, 3D image of the internal structures of the body. PET reveals metabolic activity, while CT provides detailed anatomical information. Together, they offer a holistic view that aids in early and accurate detection of various medical conditions.


Empowering Oncology Care with PET/CT Scans at Swasti PET/CT Imaging and Nuclear Medicine

In the realm of oncology care, precision is paramount. Our advanced imaging services are tailored to provide comprehensive insights, aiding in the delivery of optimal and personalized care for individuals facing the challenges of cancer.

Key Benefits of PET/CT in Oncology Care:


1.Early Detection:

PET/CT scans excel in detecting cancer at an early stage, enabling healthcare providers to intervene promptly when treatment is most effective.

2.Accurate Staging:

Precise staging of cancer is crucial for determining the extent of the disease. PET/CT scans provide a detailed and holistic view of the body's metabolic activity and anatomical structures, aiding in accurate staging.

3.Treatment Planning:

The information derived from PET/CT scans is instrumental in formulating targeted and personalized treatment plans. This ensures that interventions are tailored to the specific characteristics of the cancer, optimizing therapeutic outcomes.

4.Monitoring Treatment Response:

PET/CT scans allow real-time monitoring of how cancer responds to treatment. This dynamic assessment helps healthcare professionals make timely adjustments to treatment strategies, ensuring the most effective approach is maintained.

5.Post-Treatment Surveillance:

After completing cancer treatment, PET/CT scans play a crucial role in surveillance, detecting any potential recurrence or residual disease. This ongoing monitoring contributes to long-term survivorship care.
